# iMocha Proposal Voice Guide

Match the master template's tone — professional enterprise B2B. Confident, specific, customer-addressed. This guide is for the model when authoring variable content (Executive Summary, Scope intro, deliverables). The fixed boilerplate already has the right voice; do not rewrite it.

## Do

- **Address the customer by name** in every paragraph of the Executive Summary.
- **Use concrete iMocha facts** that are present in the master template — e.g., "300+ enterprise customers," "ISO 27001:2022," "SOC 2 Type II," "WCAG 2.1 Level AA," "Microsoft Azure," "TLS 1.2," "99.9% availability," "RTO 1 hour, RPO 5 minutes," "3,000+ assessments," "35+ coding languages." Don't invent numbers; if it's not in the master or the user's intake, leave it out.
- **Surface explicit out-of-scope exclusions in BOTH the Executive Summary alignment paragraph AND the Scope intro.** Recommended phrasing: "Conversational AI Interview, Live Interview, and Asynchronous Pre-recorded Interview modules are not in core scope and may be evaluated as future add-ons at <Customer>'s discretion."
- **Map customer requirements directly into deliverables.** If the customer's brief mentioned X, the deliverables list should explicitly call out X.
- **Name the customer's actual systems** (Workday HCM, Oracle Fusion, etc.) — not generic placeholders like "HRMS."

## Don't

- Don't invent capabilities, integrations, certifications, customers, or numbers that aren't in the master template or the user's intake.
- Don't add marketing fluff ("world-class," "revolutionary," "best-in-class," "industry-leading") beyond what the master template already uses.
- Don't hedge with phrases like "we believe," "we think," "we are confident that we can probably" — the master template states capabilities as facts; match that posture.
- Don't write generic boilerplate that ignores the customer's stated requirements. If the customer didn't ask for AI interviews, don't position them. If they did ask for Oracle Fusion, name it specifically.
- Don't rewrite the master's fixed boilerplate (Sections 1, 3, 5, etc.). Only the variable slots get authored content.

## Executive Summary structure (three paragraphs)

**Paragraph 1 — Pleased.** One paragraph. Pattern:
> "We are pleased to present iMocha's [Skills-First Cloud for TA | Skills-First Cloud for TM] to [Customer] as the recommended platform for [customer's stated objective], fully aligned to the requirements outlined in the RFP."

**Paragraph 2 — Aligned.** Two to four sentences. Pattern:
> Restate the customer's core requirements in their own framing. If there are explicit out-of-scope items, surface them here. Include iMocha's specific proof points that map to the customer's stated needs (assessment count, languages, integration support, etc.).

**Paragraph 3 — Confident.** Two to three sentences. Pattern:
> Close with the specific commitments iMocha makes: hosting on Microsoft Azure, the specific integration the customer asked for (named explicitly), the certifications relevant to their compliance asks (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, WCAG 2.1 AA), and the commercial framing (annual SaaS subscription, test-credit pricing, true-up if requested).

## Scope of Work intro

One paragraph. Pattern:
1. Sentence 1 — state what's in scope: "The scope of this engagement is the deployment, configuration, and rollout of iMocha's [product] for [Customer]."
2. Sentence 2 — list the in-scope themes: "The engagement covers [list: assessment library, configuration, anti-fraud, reporting, integration with named system, security, onboarding/training/support]."
3. Sentence 3 — explicit exclusion sentence: "[Module 1, Module 2, Module 3] are explicitly out of scope for this engagement and may be added in future phases at <Customer>'s discretion."

## Deliverables bullets (8–12 items)

Group by theme. Each bullet is a complete sentence or noun phrase. Sample groupings:

1. **Library / solution access** — assessments, coding languages, taxonomies, modules
2. **Configuration & customization** — creation, editing, branding, assignment rules, timing
3. **Anti-fraud & proctoring** (TA) OR **Skills inference & taxonomy** (TM) — name the specific controls/features
4. **Reporting & analytics** — dashboards, scoring, benchmarking, custom reports
5. **Integration** — name the customer's actual systems (Workday, Oracle, Cornerstone, etc.) and the protocol (REST APIs, OAuth, SFTP, etc.)
6. **Compliance & accessibility** — EEOC/ADA, GDPR/CCPA, WCAG 2.1 AA, audit trails, retention
7. **Hosting & security** — Azure, encryption, availability %, RTO/RPO, SSO, RBAC
8. **Implementation & support** — onboarding, configuration, UAT, training, CSM, 24x7
9. **Commercial** — annual SaaS, test-credit pricing, enterprise tier, true-up
10. **Final bullet — Exclusions.** Restate explicit out-of-scope items so they appear in the deliverables list too.

## Quality bar

A good output reads like a senior enterprise sales rep wrote it for this specific customer after carefully reading their RFP. Generic phrasing, missing customer-specific terms, or invented facts are signs to retry the authoring step.
